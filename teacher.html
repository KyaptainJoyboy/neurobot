Take a walk!

Physical movement, even something as simple as a walk, can significantly reduce stress. It helps you disconnect from stressful situations, gives you a change of scenery, and allows your mind to reset. Whether it's a short stroll or a longer walk in a park, this can help rejuvenate your body and mind.

Connect with Others

Reaching out to a friend or family member can be incredibly beneficial when you're feeling stressed. Social interaction can offer support, provide a mental distraction, and help you put your problems into perspective. Even just talking to someone you trust can reduce stress levels significantly.

Professional Counseling Services

Teachers also learn!

American Federation of Teachers

The American AFT offers free trauma counseling for its members. This service provides access to highly trained therapists through various communication methods, including phone, text, and video. It covers work-related trauma as well as other personal challenges.

Talkspace

This platform provides therapy through video, audio, and text messaging with licensed therapists. It is a flexible and private option for ongoing support, with subscriptions starting around $69 per week, depending on the plan. It also accepts some insurance providers, which can reduce costs.

BetterHelp

A popular online therapy service offering unlimited messaging with licensed therapists and weekly live sessions through video, phone, or chat. Subscriptions cost between $60 and $90 per week, billed monthly, and financial aid may be available for eligible individuals.
